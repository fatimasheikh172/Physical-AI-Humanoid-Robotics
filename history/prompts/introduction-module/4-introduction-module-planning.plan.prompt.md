# Introduction Module Planning: Foundations of Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## Context

This PHR documents the implementation planning for the Introduction Module of the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics textbook. The module establishes conceptual, mathematical, and engineering foundations required to understand Physical AI, embodied intelligence, humanoid robotics, and the transition from digital AI systems to real-world autonomous robotic agents.

## Prompt Text

```
INTRODUCTION MODULE NAME:
Foundations of Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

INTRODUCTION MODULE DURATION:
2 Weeks (Weeks 1–2 of the Quarter)

INTRODUCTION MODULE PURPOSE:
This introduction module establishes the conceptual, mathematical, and engineering foundations required to understand Physical AI, embodied intelligence, humanoid robotics, and the transition from digital AI systems to real-world autonomous robotic agents.

By the end of this module, the student will clearly understand:
- What Physical AI is
- What embodied intelligence means
- How humanoid robots differ from traditional robots
- How AI connects with physical sensors, motors, and environments

=====================================================================
WEEK 1 PLAN – INTRODUCTION TO PHYSICAL AI & EMBODIED INTELLIGENCE

LEARNING OBJECTIVES:
- Understand the meaning of Physical AI
- Understand the difference between Digital AI and Physical AI
- Learn what Embodied Intelligence means
- Understand why humanoid robots are important
- Learn where Physical AI is used in the real world

THEORY CONTENT TO DEVELOP:
- What is Artificial Intelligence
- What is Physical AI
- Digital Brain vs Physical Body concept
- What is Embodied Intelligence
- Evolution of robots: Industrial → Service → Humanoid
- Difference between software agents and physical agents
- Human-centered environments and humanoid advantage

HANDS-ON LAB TASKS:
- Virtual observation of humanoid robots in simulation videos
- Identification of sensors and actuators in humanoid robots
- Mapping human senses to robot sensors

WEEK 1 MINI PROJECT:
- Create a concept report:
  Student selects one real-world robot (Tesla Optimus, Atlas, Pepper, etc.)
  Student explains:
  - Sensors used
  - AI system used
  - Physical actions performed

WEEK 1 ASSESSMENT:
- MCQs on Physical AI concepts
- Short written explanation of Embodied Intelligence

AI TUTOR INTEGRATION:
- AI explains Physical AI concepts in beginner language
- AI answers student conceptual questions
- AI provides real-world robot examples

=====================================================================
WEEK 2 PLAN – HUMANOID ROBOTICS LANDSCAPE & SENSOR SYSTEMS

LEARNING OBJECTIVES:
- Understand what a humanoid robot is
- Learn humanoid robot structure
- Understand robot sensors and actuators
- Learn how robots perceive the physical world

THEORY CONTENT TO DEVELOP:
- What is a humanoid robot
- Human-like kinematics vs wheeled robots
- Degrees of freedom in humanoids
- Vision systems: Cameras, Depth Cameras
- Range sensors: LiDAR, Ultrasonic
- Motion sensors: IMU
- Force and torque sensors
- Actuators: Servos, Motors

HANDS-ON LAB TASKS:
- Virtual sensor exploration in simulation
- Observation of sensor data outputs
- Identify sensors used in humanoid robots

WEEK 2 MINI PROJECT:
- Design a basic humanoid sensor layout:
  - Head: Vision sensors
  - Body: IMU
  - Hands: Force sensors
  - Legs: Motion sensors

WEEK 2 ASSESSMENT:
- MCQs on humanoid structure and sensor systems
- Practical assignment on sensor identification

AI TUTOR INTEGRATION:
- AI explains sensor working principles
- AI compares sensors based on use-case
- AI helps students design sensor layouts

=====================================================================
INTRODUCTION MODULE FINAL OUTPUT DELIVERABLES:

- Complete Introduction Chapter for the Textbook
- Physical AI & Embodied Intelligence Theory Sections
- Humanoid Robotics Overview Section
- Sensor & Actuator Fundamentals Section
- 2 Weekly Mini Projects
- 2 Weekly Assessments
- AI Tutor Enabled for Concept Learning
```
